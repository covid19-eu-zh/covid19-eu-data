Daily reports about coronavirus disease (COVID-19)

Published 12.03.2020 Updated 10.05.2020

Here is a selection of data from the daily status reports about coronavirus disease (COVID-19) in Norway.

The daily pdf report will not be published during the weekend 9./10. of May. The diagrams below will be next updated on Monday.

  • The total number of confirmed COVID-19 cases is 8099, according to the Norwegian Surveillance System for Communicable Diseases (MSIS) at 24:00 the 9. of May. Be aware that the number may be adjusted retroactively when the daily report is next published.
  • The total number of COVID-19 deaths reported to the Norwegian Institute of Public Health is 217. Updated at 09:00 the 10. Of May.
  • Number of people admitted to hospital, see Directorate of Health.

Laboratory analyses 

The number of laboratory analyses with analyses results are now collected from the National Laboratory database. Analyses results reported on the testing date are copied from laboratories that test for SARS-CoV-2 directly to the database.

Number of confirmed cases in Norway per day

The figure below shows the number of cases reported daily in Norway since the start of the epidemic. The cases are registered by the date when the tests were performed. There are 1-2 days delay in the time from diagnosis to registration in the Norwegian Surveillance System for Communicable Diseases (MSIS). The number of cases is updated retroactively when new notifications arrive. 

Age and gender 

The diagram below shows the total number of confirmed cases in Norway since the epidemic began, by age and gender. The average age is 46 years, 50 per cent are male. 

COVID-19 associated deaths

The average age is 82 years, the median age is 84 and 55 % are male. Patients may have died from or with COVID-19.

R (reproduction number)

The modelling group at the NIPH publish daily reports about the situational analysis and forecasting. The reports include the latest calculation of the R (reproduction number), i.e. how many people infected by an infected person, on average.  

  • Coronavirus modelling at the NIPH - see reports, table 1 in the pdf file shows R.

COVID-19 statistics

Total mortality

The NIPH also calculates the total mortality in Norway through the NorMOMO-system. The status for total mortality is mentioned in the weekly reports.

  • Latest diagram for European countries in EuroMOMO

Reproduction number - R

The modelling group at NIPH publishes daily reports with situational analyses and forecasting. The reports also include the latest calculation of the reproduction number, R, which shows how many people are infected by an infected person on average.

  • Coronavirus modelling at NIPH - see pdf reports at the bottom of the page, table 1 in the report shows R.
